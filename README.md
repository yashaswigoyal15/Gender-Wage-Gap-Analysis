This project examines the gender wage gap across eight countries — Brazil, France, India, the Philippines, Sri Lanka, Switzerland, Thailand, and the United States — spanning six sectors and the period 2018 to 2023. Using monthly earnings data drawn from the International Labour Organization (ILO), the analysis reveals substantial heterogeneity in wage disparities across both national and sectoral dimensions. Brazil records the highest mean gap (~21.2%), while the Philippines shows near parity on aggregate (~1.6%) and exhibits a female earnings advantage in several sectors. Financial services and manufacturing emerge as the most persistently unequal sectors globally. Overall, the gap narrowed modestly between 2021 and 2022, but resurged in 2023, underscoring the structural rather than transitory nature of wage inequality.
